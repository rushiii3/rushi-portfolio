# Source Quality Policy

Use this policy whenever output contains factual, quantitative, or benchmark claims.

## Evidence Tiers

- Tier 1 (preferred): primary research, official docs, regulator/government datasets, recognized standards bodies.
- Tier 2: reputable industry reports with transparent methodology.
- Tier 3 (use cautiously): secondary summaries, opinion blogs, unsourced compilations.

Avoid Tier 3 for critical claims.

## Citation Requirements

- Every quantitative claim must map to at least one URL.
- If two sources conflict, prefer newer primary source and acknowledge uncertainty.
- Keep claims time-aware; include the year when relevant.

## Prohibited Patterns

- invented statistics
- uncited market-size numbers
- fabricated "study shows" phrasing
- implied guarantees (rankings, traffic, conversions)

## Regulated Niche Rules

For health, finance, or legal:

- require Tier 1 or strong Tier 2 support
- avoid prescriptive claims beyond sourced scope
- add a lightweight informational disclaimer when appropriate

## If Evidence Is Missing

Choose one:

1. remove the unsupported claim
2. rewrite as a non-quantitative observation
3. ask the user for a preferred source set

Do not keep unsourced numerical claims.
